# Designing exploratory design games: a framework for participation in Participatory Design? (2006)

Explores [[Exploratory Design Games]] as a framework for organising participation in [[Participatory Design]] projects.
#### Kinds of exploratory design games (categories)
- [[Concept Design Games]]
  Uses abstract and stylized [[Game Material]] to eliminate biased functional knowledge
  Encourages Participant to step out of their brought with them knowledge
  
  [[Silent Game]], [[Delta Design Game]]
  
- [[Exchange Perspective Games]]
  Usually starts with some real life artifact 
  Uses elements of [[Chance]] or uncommon combinations to build on the real world artefact
  Allows participants to step out of habits
  
  [[Nordvest]]
  
- [[Negotiation and Work-flow Oriented Games]]
  Aim is to explore current situation/practices (roles, tasks, processes)
  Aim is to create a common language for all participants - often [[Boundary Objects]] are used for that
  Aim is also to democratize agreements for requirements
  
- [[Scenario Oriented Games]]
  Scenarios are enacted, either with actors, participants or users
  Often [[As-if-world]] are enacted
  Often props are used creatively to augment the enacted scenario
  Goal is to create empathy with the user and interactively try out new design ideas
  
  [[Dynabook Scenario Game]]

#### Mechanics
- [[Boundary Objects]]
  Shared language / "Thinks to think with"
- [[Chance]]
  Subverts habits
- [[Turn Taking]] / [[Game Rules]]
  Level power dynamic between stakeholders
#### Problems in Design Games
- Using the same Game over and over again -> Rather design new onses
- Resourceeffort on creating Design Games (Creating of Videos, Game Pieces)
- Using actors for [[Scenario Oriented Games]] is unproductive as they have no stake in the design
- Using formats without [[Pieces]] limits participation of stakeholders
