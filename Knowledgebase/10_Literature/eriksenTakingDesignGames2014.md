# Taking design games seriously: re-connecting situated power relations of people and materials (2014)

Explores the power dynamic between material parts of design-games and the human participants on the example of a [[UrbanTransition Game]] playthrough by using concepts from the [[Actor-Network-Theory]]

Identifies the following Power Problems
- [[Challenge - Gamemasters]] - Often PD Researchers (who were part of designing the game) act as Gamemasters. This creates a power shift towards the researchers
- [[Challenge - Agenda Control]] - Gamemasters can preselect which Game items to include and thus influence what discussions arise. Participants felt manipulated through this. 
- [[Challenge - Participants with knowledge]] - Having [[Attachments]] - Participants that have indepth knowledge about a feel (e.g. Park services) can shift power dynamics in discussions by displaying this knowledge

Mentioned Remedies for Power Problems are
- Having a non PD Researcher act as Gamemaster is mentioned as a way to level out that Power dynamic issue
- Predescussing with the Participants which part to include might level out the Agenda power dynamic issue
- Turn Taking - Is described as a way to level out inequal power dynamics between participants
