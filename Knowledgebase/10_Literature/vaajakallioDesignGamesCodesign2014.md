# Design games in codesign: as a tool, a mindset and a structure (2014)

Main contribution: introduction of a play framework

Categorizes design games by purpose
- **For research** Study design actions in a manipulable environment. 
  Players moves are limited by rules, convention and principles
  
  Connects those games to [[1980_ Habraken - Concept Design Games]]
  Thus most likely similar to [[Concept Design Games]]
  
- **for building design competence**  create, play and reflect game playing as a way of teaching students how to establish interaction between stakeholders in design processes
  
- **for empowering users** provide tools to establish common language between users and designers. Involve users in discussions. Historically also dealt with improving democratisations of workplaces. 
  
  Possibly similar to [[Negotiation and Work-flow Oriented Games]]
  
- **for engaging multiple stakeholders** engage stakeholders in expressing, negotiating, generating a shared understand of users, use contexts and technology in early concept design. Paper focuses on this category. 
  
  Bases it on [[brandtFacilitatingCollaborationDesign2004a]]
