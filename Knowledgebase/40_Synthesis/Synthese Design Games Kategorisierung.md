[[brandtDesigningExploratoryDesign2006]] categorizes [[Exploratory Design Games]] based on their function in relation to [[Codesign]]:
- [[Concept Design Games]]
- [[Exchange Perspective Games]]
- [[Negotiation and Work-flow Oriented Games]]
- [[Scenario Oriented Games]]

Those four functions also influence the way a particular design game looks and feels like. [[vaajakallioDesignGamesCodesign2014]]

[[vaajakallioDesignGamesCodesign2014]] builds on [[brandtDesigningExploratoryDesign2006]] and categorizes [[Design Games]] by the [[Purpose for employing design games]]:
- [[For research]]
- [[For building design competence]]
- [[For empowering users]]
- [[For engaging multiple stakeholders]]

Design games often imply more than one of the four functions introduced by Brandt. [[vaajakallioDesignGamesCodesign2014]] proposes a listing where the following core functions are shared by most [[Design Games]]:
- [[creating a common design language]]
- [[promoting a creative and explorative attitude]]
- [[facilitating the players in envisioning and enacting 'what could be']]
- [[helping to define the roles of participants in the interaction during a session]]
