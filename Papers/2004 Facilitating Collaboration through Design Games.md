### Scenario Based Design
Constructing Scenarios as a vehicle for interaction. Scenarios have the advantage of being both concrete and flexible [2000 Carroll]. Provides interaction designer with a language for expressing design ideas that to some extent corresponds to sketching (e.g. for architects). 

Examples: 
Experience Prototyping [Buchenau] Explorative experiments of interacting with mock-ups, prototypes or existing products to convey the subjective experience to designers/future users. 

Body Storming (Similar to Forum Theatre or Focus Troupe [1999 Sato] oder [Howard]). 
SPECS - User is provided with simple mock-up of future device, then followed through their day where the mockup is used to envision ideas of services and features of the product.
### Design is a Game
[2004 Bucciarelli] Playing Games and designing are both social acts and based on rules. Like in games, members have different areas of responsibility and expertise and thus roles. Important part of the development process is that the designers communicate and negotiate with each other. Roles set boundaries for what is possible and structure the play of the game. 

Examples: _Layout Kit_ where carpenters used cards representing machines to rework the layout of the carpentry. _Organizational Kit_ where cards were used to reconstruct the workflow of a newspaper production facility. 

Main uses of the games have been to engage workers in the process where they can create a common language [Much like Domain Driven Design]?, discuss existing reality, investigate future visions. 

System descriptions produced in the games: Focus is not if they are correct but on how they make sense to all participants. 

-> Focus in the project of [Ehn, Sjögren] was the empowerment of workers
### Example Games
Have been central to help facilitate a user-centered design process. The games are not about winning but about trying out and exploring various aspects of design. 

They propose 4 Games that build on each other: 
User Game - players create stories about users and emerge themself in the user world
Landscape Game - Context is created for people portrayed in the User game
Technology Game - technologies and form factor are introduced
Scenario Game - Experiences from former games are condensed in scenarios and enacted
#### User Game
