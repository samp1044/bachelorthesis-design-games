**Standford d.school model (dschool.standford.edu)**
By Hasso Plattner Institute
Structured around five iterative stages:
- Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype and Test
Emphasizes human-centered approach and iterative problem-solving
Empathy as a guiding principle

Also in the history the author mentions connections to: 
- IDEO (David Kelley 2001): Empathy, iterative prototyping, multidisciplinary collaboration. 
- One of the most widely adopted models

**Double Diamond (Online Resource)**
introduced 2004 and revised 2015 
Outlines process as two successive phases of divergent and convergent thinking:
- Discover, Define, Develop and Deliver
Visually reinforces the importance of broad problem exploration, followed by focused solution development
Later refinements have incorporated interdisciplinary collaboration and system-level thinking. 
Distinctly visualizes the process of divergence and convergence

**IDEO human-centered design model**
IDEO (David Kelley, then Tim Brown) 
approach is structured into three phases: 
- Inspiration, Ideation, Implementation
Particular emphasis on deep user empathy, rapid prototyping and interdisciplinary teamwork
Greater Focus on Stages Inspiration and Implementation
While initially intended for business innovation, has since been successfully applied to address health, education and development challenges

Share foundational principles: focus on user needs, iterative development, interdisciplinary collaboration, balance between divergent and convergent thinking. 

At other place: Models converge around four foundational principles: user-centricity, iterative prototyping, creative ideation, interdisciplinary collaboration

Unexplored areas in Design Thinking: 
Key shortcoming is the limited focus on impact assessment. Much of previous research emphasizes design processes and immediate outputs, with insufficient attention to long-term effects or measurable outcomes. 

Shortcomings of Design Thinking Methods (possible relevance for Design Games as well):
- Research has limited focus on impact assessment -> Emphasize on Design Process and Immediate outputs
- Limited use of DT in public sectors: Possible barriers: organizational intertia, risk aversion, hierarchical cultures and tension between DT experimental ethos and compliance driven gov models
- Ehtical considerations largely overlooked (e.g. issues of stakeholder inclusions, power asymmetries)
- Some scholars critize the rise of DT as a managerial trend lacking methodological rigor and measurable outcomes (= Design Games benutzt als echtes Erkentnisswerkzeug oder nur als Icebreaker)
