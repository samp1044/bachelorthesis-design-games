Literature review about how Design Thinking creates Impact and on what theoretical foundations that impact might be based on. 

Identifies four theoretical mechanisms that explain the impact of Design Thinking
- Integration
  Novel combinations of different strands of knowledge
  Making sense of different elements through novel combinations. 
  By balancing Exploration and Exploitation
  Exploration through observation and interviews, empathic dialogue with users and curious experimentation, asking open questions
  Exploiting existing knowledge of team members, combining expertise from different departments and backgrounds
	- Possible Design Games:
	  User Game (Personas -> vielleicht eher Collaborative); Franken Ideation
- Reframing
  "Putting on glasses to look at a situation/problem in different light"
  Proactive reorganization of information that changes the perception of situations or experiences
  Key mechanisms: cognitive bias reduction, diverse knowledge paradigms, abductive logic (Schließe auf die beste Erklärung)
	- Possible Design Games:
	  Roleplaying; 5 Ws and H; Challenge cards/Perspective shift
- Enablement
  Empowerment, Flow, Self-efficacy, Experiential learning
	- Possible Design Games:
	  Yes, and (Improv)
- Collaborative Engagement
  Defined as involvement with others in order to achieve a better understanding or a better outcome
	- Possible Design Games
	  Speed Boat, Empathy Mapping Board, User Game?

Defines DT as human-centered approach that puts human needs right at the forefront of the innovation process. Others describe it as a practice. Paper considers it as a practice that bundles attitudes, tools and approaches. 

Two main phases underlie this process (basically Double Diamond):
- Problem Space
  Understand the problem through need-finding with users and stakeholders
- Solution Space
  Iteratively create solutions for the problem
Every phase consists of opening (Divergenz) and closing (Konvergenz). 

Mentions that scholars agree that core elements of DT include:
- identifying human needs
- ideation
- prototyping
